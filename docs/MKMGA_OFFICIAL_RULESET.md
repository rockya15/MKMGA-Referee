**The Cascade Rule:**

When a participant is assigned DNF (either as their sole remaining pick or as one of many picks), they have two options:

(i) Accept DNF — retain the position. No further action.

(ii) Cascade — invoke the DNF cascade wheel.

**Cascade Spending Rules:**
- Only **one cascade chain per position assignment phase** can ever be triggered when 13 or fewer participants have paid
- A cascade chain is considered complete (spent) when it ends via any of: a participant lands on a free position, a participant lands on DNF, or a participant is forced to accept DNF by The Wheel
- Once a chain completes, **no further cascades may be started** in that round — any subsequent DNF assignments must be accepted

**Cascade Trigger Consequences:**

The cascade wheel's starting probability depends on ***whether the current participant was forced to take DNF or chose it voluntarily:***

**Forced DNF (Gentle Cascade Table):** The participant had zero other options available. All other positions are taken or unavailable. This participant is forced into DNF. They may accept it or cascade. They start at **level 1** of the gentle table.

**Chosen DNF (Harsh Start, ~31% base):** The participant had other available positions but deliberately selected DNF. This is a gamble — either accept DNF, or cascade. If they cascade, they're betting on The Wheel to displace another participant into DNF, creating chaos. **The punishment:** they begin at ~31% probability.

If this early cascader succeeds and displaces another participant into DNF, that displaced participant enters the **gentle cascade table at the next level** — not restarting harsh. This localizes the penalty to the scummy early-picker.

Used **only by the participant who initiated the cascade** by choosing DNF when alternatives existed.

| Spin | DNF Slots | Position Slots | DNF Chance |

**Strategic Implication:**
There is only one cascade per position assignment phase. If you select DNF early and cascade, you start at harsh ~31%. If you succeed and displace another participant, that participant enters the gentle cascade at level 2 (~15%), continuing from where you left off. This incentivizes participants to either:
- Take their preferred position and leave DNF for the last participant (who starts at gentle level 1, ~8%), or
- Accept DNF immediately if forced (enters gentle level 1), preserving cascade for another participant

**Within a Cascade Chain:**
- Landing on a free position ends the chain — the cascading participant takes it, equilibrium restored
- Landing on a taken position displaces that participant to DNF (they may accept it or, if the cascade chain has not yet ended, trigger it themselves at the next gentle level)
- Landing on DNF ends the chain unconditionally — the cascading participant must accept
- At gentle level 5 or harsh spin 3, DNF is guaranteed — the chain always ends
- If a participant using the harsh cascade is displaced back to DNF by a downstream participant reclaiming their position, they must continue their harsh cascade progression, not switch to gentle logic. This means they face increasing DNF probabilities on subsequent spins, potentially leading to guaranteed DNF.

ARTICLE VI — THE BETTING PHASE

Section 6.01 — Commencement of Betting

Upon completion of Position Assignment for all paying participants, the Betting Phase shall commence. Participants shall act in Wheel Order — that is, in the order in which they were selected by The Wheel during Position Assignment. The first selected participant acts first.

Section 6.02 — Available Actions

During a participant's turn, they may select from the following actions, subject to availability as governed by game state:

(a) CHECK — The participant passes without contributing additional funds. Available only if no Raise has occurred during the current betting round. A Check is not a retreat. A Check is a tactical pause. A Check can also mean you have nothing and are hoping everyone else folds.

(b) RAISE — The participant increases the current required bet by a coin-friendly increment ($0.25, $0.50, $1.00, $2.00, or a custom coin-friendly amount). Upon a Raise, all participants who acted before the raising participant are re-queued and must Call or Fold before the round closes. Re-queued participants may not raise again. Only participants who have not yet acted in the current raise cycle may raise.

(c) CALL — The participant matches the current required bet in full. If the participant's total remaining balance is insufficient to match the bet, they must either Fold or go All In.

(d) FOLD — The participant withdraws from the current race's pot. All funds previously contributed by the folding participant to The Pot are permanently forfeited. There are no refunds. There is no undo button. There is no appeals process. Folding is final.

Section 6.03 — The Bet Cap

The maximum permissible cumulative bet for any given betting round shall be equal to the remaining balance of the lowest-funded paying participant at the start of the Betting Phase — that is, their balance after paying the Entry Fee but before any bets have been placed. Participants who did not pay the Entry Fee are excluded from this calculation. Once the cumulative bet reaches this cap:

(a) The Raise option shall be removed from the interface of all remaining participants.
(b) Participants who have not yet matched the current bet may only Call or Fold.
(c) Participants who have already matched the bet may only Check or Fold.
(d) Raising stops entirely; betting continues only as Calls, Checks, or Folds.

The Bet Cap is recalculated **only if a participant folds.** Going All In does not recalculate the cap upward. Example: Participant 1 (floor at $2, $2 remaining) folds after Participant 2 raises to $3. Participant 3 can now raise higher than Participant 2 could, but Participant 2 (who already matched at $3) is closed out of the action and cannot re-enter even though the cap changed. A participant completing a call by going All In does not get re-queued; their action is finalized.

Section 6.04 — All In Provisions

A participant who cannot meet the current required bet must either Fold (subject to token availability per Section 4.02(c)) or commit their entire remaining balance as an All In. Going All In does not grant the participant any additional rights, advantages, sympathy, or immunity from The Wheel.

Section 6.05 — The Sixty-Second Timer and Group Vote Protocol

Each participant shall have sixty (60) seconds to complete their betting action. A timer shall be displayed prominently on The TV and on the participant's mobile device. The timer will turn red in the final fifteen (15) seconds. This is intentional. This is pressure. This is the game.

6.05(a) — Timeout Consequences

Upon expiration of the sixty-second timer without a completed action, a thirty (30) second Group Vote shall be initiated among all other active participants. During the Group Vote:

1. All other active participants shall be presented with the available actions for the timed-out participant, specifically those permissible given current game state (e.g., Check/Fold or Call/Fold).
2. Each voting participant casts one vote.
3. The majority vote is executed on behalf of the timed-out participant.
4. In the event of a tie, The Host shall cast the deciding vote.
5. The timed-out participant's device shall display a notification that a vote is in progress on their behalf.
6. The timed-out participant has no recourse. The group has spoken.
7. In the event the Group Vote results in a Fold, said Fold shall be treated as any voluntary Fold for purposes of the Skip/Fold Token defined in Section 4.02(c). The timed-out participant's token is consumed. They did this to themselves.

6.05(b) — Disconnect During Voting

In the event a participant disconnects during their active turn, their turn shall be held for thirty (30) seconds to permit reconnection. If the participant reconnects within this window, they are returned to their active prompt. If they do not reconnect within thirty (30) seconds, the Group Vote Protocol is initiated as described in Section 6.05(a). Upon reconnection after a completed Group Vote, the participant shall be returned to the idle state, as no further action is required of them.

AMENDMENT A.01 — FREE-CHOICE POSITION SELECTION

(Modifies: Article V, Section 5.02 — Operation of The Wheel; and Article V, Section 5.04(a) — More Than Thirteen Paying Participants. Also see Article VI, Section 6.01 — Commencement of Betting.)

Section 5.02 operates as written when the number of paying participants is thirteen (13) or fewer. However, when the number of paying participants exceeds thirteen (13) — the condition governed by Section 5.04(a) — The Wheel shall not be used for position assignment. The following Free-Choice Protocol applies instead:

(a) Upon commencement of the Position Assignment phase with more than thirteen (13) paying participants, all paying participants may select their desired position at any time of their own choosing. There is no waiting for The Wheel. There is no spin. The Wheel watches silently from the sidelines. It does not interfere.

(b) The official Betting Order for the subsequent Betting Phase shall be determined not by Wheel spin sequence, but by the order in which participants submit their position selections. The first participant to choose is the first to bet. The last participant to choose is the last to bet. This replaces the Wheel Order provision referenced in Section 6.01 for this scenario only.

(c) In all other respects, Section 5.04(a) remains in effect: positions are non-exclusive, multiple participants may hold the same position, shared winning positions split the pot equally, and DNF is freely selectable without cascade.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

AMENDMENT A.02 — REFUSAL TO CONTINUE CONSTITUTES ELIMINATION

(Modifies: Article VIII, Section 8.04(b) — Voluntary Withdrawal.)

Section 8.04(b) identifies Voluntary Withdrawal as a valid edge-case elimination condition. This Amendment clarifies and extends that provision as follows:

At any point between races when participants are asked, formally or informally, whether they wish to continue play, any participant who does not affirmatively agree to continue — whether by explicit refusal, silence, physical departure, non-responsiveness, or a general vibe of not being in it anymore — shall be deemed to have voluntarily withdrawn from play under Section 8.04(b) and shall be immediately and irrevocably eliminated.

A participant's failure to engage is itself a decision. The game recognizes it as such. The game has seen it before. The game is not surprised.

Any remaining balance held by a participant eliminated under this provision is immediately forfeited to The Pot in accordance with the remainder of Section 8.04. There are no grace periods, no second chances, and no retroactive opt-ins. If you needed a moment, you should have said so before it was your moment.

AMENDMENT A.03 — REVISED BET CAP FORMULA

(Modifies: Article VI, Section 6.03 — The Bet Cap.)

Section 6.03 is hereby amended in its entirety with respect to how the Bet Cap is calculated. The Bet Cap shall no longer equal the full remaining balance of the lowest-funded paying participant. Instead, the following formula and reset conditions apply:

(a) Primary Formula — The Bet Cap shall equal one half (½) of the remaining balance of the lowest-funded paying participant at the start of the Betting Phase, calculated after the Entry Fee has been paid but before any bets have been placed. If this calculation produces a result that is not a multiple of $0.25, it shall be rounded down to the nearest quarter. The game does not deal in fractions of quarters. No one does.

(b) Zero-Balance Reset — In the event that the lowest-funded paying participant has a remaining balance of exactly $0.00 at the start of the Betting Phase — meaning their entire balance was consumed by the Entry Fee and they enter the Betting Phase All In — the Bet Cap shall be recalculated using the remaining balance of the second-lowest-funded paying participant, applying the same one-half formula described in subsection (a) above.

(c) All other mechanics of Section 6.03 remain in effect: the Raise option is removed once the cumulative bet reaches the Cap, the Cap is recalculated if a participant folds, and participants who have already matched the current bet are not re-queued due to a Cap change.
